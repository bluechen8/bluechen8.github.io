Research

  • Evict+Skip: A High-Bandwidth, Non-Shared-Page Cache Covert Channel Attack, July 2021 – Oct 2021
    MIT CSAIL, Advisor: Prof. Mengjia Yan

    • Explored and reproduced the state-of-the-art asynchronous covert channel named Streamline with the non-inclusive machine (Cascade Lake).

    • Proposed a novel covert channel attack called Evict+Skip that leverages the directory conflicts of non-inclusive machine and the universal feature of Prime+Probe Attack to overcome the shared-virtual-memory limitation and even further improve the bandwidth.

    • The main challenges refer to tuning a variety of parameters for adjusting the speed of sender and receiver and taking advantage of memory accessing parallel to speed up processes while stabilizing the communication procedure.

    • Through theoretical analysis, Evict+Skip can achieve higher bandwidth than Streamline.

  • Intelligent Video Analysis in the Intelligent Power Plant, Apr 2020 – May 2021
    36th Provincial Scientific Research Training Program (SRTP), Zhejiang University, China, Advisor: Prof. Qiang Yang

    • Developed a system consisting of helmet object detection, multi-objective trace (workers) and face recognition that is able to meet the security requirement of the intelligent power plant, such as stranger invasion and worker's unsafe behavior logging.

    • Through labelling the dataset crawled from Internet and augmenting the batch size, object detection part was enhanced from 75.91% mAP to 94.13% mAP.
